## About This Repository

This is an open-access teaching and research resource containing original
moot court problems, bench memos, and a complete pedagogical framework
for use in BA LLB, B.Com LLB, and LLB programmes across Indian law schools.

It is designed and maintained by Dr. Anjum Hassan — Associate Professor and
Head of Department (Law) with 13+ years of moot court teaching, competition
organisation, and national-level judging experience. The problems in this
repository are original, research-grounded, and specifically designed to
develop the analytical and advocacy skills that the Bar Council of India
and the National Education Policy 2020 identify as core competencies for
law graduates.

This repository also connects directly to two UK Registered Design Patents
(2026) in legal education technology — dispute resolution simulation tools
co-developed through international academic collaboration.

## How to Use This Resource

**For faculty**: each problem is self-contained and can be assigned as a
standalone seminar exercise, a departmental moot, or a full competition
problem. The bench memo for each problem provides the judging framework
and expected standard of advocacy — making assessment consistent and
transparent. The PEDAGOGY.md file contains a complete 4-week training
schedule ready to integrate into any BA LLB or LLB curriculum.

**For students**: read the fact pattern carefully before consulting the
issues. Research the suggested authorities independently before reading
the bench memo. The bench memo is a post-argument resource — not a
substitute for your own research.

**For law schools**: this repository is openly licensed under CC BY 4.0.
You may adapt these problems for institutional moots, internal competitions,
and teaching materials provided attribution is given.
