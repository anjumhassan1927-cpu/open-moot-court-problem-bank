# Moot Court Problem 01 — Criminal Law
## The State v. Aryan Mehta & Anr.
**Statute: Bharatiya Nyaya Sanhita 2023**
*Difficulty: Intermediate | Suitable for: BA LLB Year 3, LLB Year 2*

---

## Background Note for Participants

This problem is set in the jurisdiction of the High Court of Judicature
at Allahabad. All references to law are to Indian statutes as in force
on 1 January 2026. Participants should research the Bharatiya Nyaya
Sanhita 2023, the Bharatiya Nagarik Suraksha Sanhita 2023, and relevant
Supreme Court and High Court precedents on the issues identified below.

---

## Fact Pattern

Aryan Mehta (Accused No. 1), a software engineer aged 28, and Priya
Sharma (Accused No. 2), a social media influencer aged 25, were in a
romantic relationship for three years. Following their separation in
March 2025, Accused No. 1 used an artificial intelligence deepfake tool
to create a realistic video depicting Accused No. 2 in a compromising
situation. The video was uploaded anonymously to three social media
platforms and was viewed by approximately 4,00,000 users within 48 hours.

Accused No. 2 suffered severe psychological harm, loss of professional
contracts valued at ₹18 lakhs, and was compelled to take medical leave
from her employment. She lodged an FIR with the Noida Cyber Crime Cell.

Accused No. 1 was arrested and charged under Sections 356 (defamation),
318 (cheating), and 351 (criminal intimidation) of the Bharatiya Nyaya
Sanhita 2023, and under Sections 66E and 67A of the Information
Technology Act 2000.

Accused No. 1 contends that: (a) the video was created as private
expression and was uploaded by an unknown third party; (b) AI-generated
content does not constitute a "statement" within the meaning of Section
356 BNS; and (c) his right to free expression under Article 19(1)(a) of
the Constitution of India is engaged.

The Additional Sessions Court, Gautam Buddha Nagar convicted Accused
No. 1 on all charges and sentenced him to three years rigorous
imprisonment. The conviction is challenged before the High Court.

---

## Issues for Argument

**Issue 1 — Applicability of BNS 2023 to AI-generated content**
Whether AI-generated synthetic media (deepfake video) constitutes a
"statement" or "representation" within the meaning of Section 356 of
the Bharatiya Nyaya Sanhita 2023, and whether the mens rea requirement
of Section 356 is satisfied where the accused created but did not
personally upload the content.

**Issue 2 — Platform liability and attribution**
Whether the uploading of AI-generated defamatory content by an anonymous
third party breaks the chain of criminal causation under BNS 2023, and
whether the accused can be held criminally liable for consequences he
claims were unforeseeable at the point of creation.

**Issue 3 — Constitutional dimension**
Whether the conviction under Section 356 BNS read with Section 66E IT
Act constitutes a disproportionate restriction on the accused's right
to free expression under Article 19(1)(a) of the Constitution, and
how the court should balance this against the complainant's right to
privacy and dignity under Article 21.
