markdown# Moot Court Problem 03 — Constitutional Law
## Dr. Farrukh Nabi v. Union of India & State of Uttar Pradesh
**Article 21 + Emerging Rights — Right to Digital Identity and AI-Free Decision Making**
*Difficulty: Advanced | Suitable for: BA LLB Year 4-5, LLB Year 3*

---

## Background Note for Participants

This problem is set before a Constitution Bench of the Supreme Court
of India. It raises novel constitutional questions about the scope of
Article 21 in the context of AI-driven public administration. Participants
should research the right to privacy jurisprudence, the right to livelihood,
and emerging international frameworks on algorithmic rights.

---

## Fact Pattern

Dr. Farrukh Nabi, an Associate Professor of Medicine at a government
medical college in Uttar Pradesh, applied for promotion to the post of
Professor under a newly implemented AI-based performance evaluation
system — the Academic Performance Indicator Automated Assessment
System (APIAAS) — introduced by the State Government in January 2025.

APIAAS assigned Dr. Nabi a performance score of 41/100. His application
for promotion was automatically rejected. Upon inquiry, he was informed
that APIAAS had deducted significant points due to: (a) a two-year
gap in his publication record between 2020 and 2022 (the COVID-19
pandemic period); (b) his participation in a faculty association that
had organised a peaceful protest in 2021; and (c) a pattern of
"non-conformist communication" detected by analysis of his internal
emails (accessed without his knowledge through the university server).

Dr. Nabi was not informed of these deductions at the time of evaluation,
was not given an opportunity to respond, and was not told that his emails
had been analysed. There is no statutory basis for the APIAAS system —
it was introduced by executive order of the State Government.

He filed a writ petition under Article 32 challenging: (a) the use of
AI in promotion decisions without statutory authority; (b) the covert
monitoring of his communications; and (c) the penalisation of his
association with a faculty union.

---

## Issues for Argument

**Issue 1 — Right to livelihood and procedural fairness**
Whether the use of an AI-based evaluation system to make promotion
decisions affecting the right to livelihood under Article 21 without
statutory authority, without prior notice of evaluation criteria,
and without an opportunity to be heard, violates the principles of
natural justice and the procedural guarantee of Article 21 as
interpreted in *Maneka Gandhi v. Union of India*.

**Issue 2 — Covert surveillance and the right to privacy**
Whether the covert monitoring of Dr. Nabi's internal emails by the
State Government through the APIAAS system constitutes a violation
of his fundamental right to privacy under Article 21 as established
in *K.S. Puttaswamy v. Union of India*, and whether any state
interest justifies such surveillance in the absence of a lawful
interception order under the Indian Telegraph Act 1885 or the
IT Act 2000.

**Issue 3 — Right to freedom of association**
Whether the penalisation of the Petitioner's membership of a faculty
association and his participation in a lawful protest through an
AI performance evaluation system violates his right to freedom of
association under Article 19(1)(c) of the Constitution, and whether
a government employer can lawfully use AI to monitor and penalise
protected associational activity.
